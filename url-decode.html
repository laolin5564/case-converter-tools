Frequently Asked Questions

What is %20 in a URL?

%20 is the URL-encoded representation of a space character. Other common encodings include %3D (=), %26 (&), %3F (?), and %23 (#).

Does it decode plus signs as spaces?

Yes! In URL query strings, plus signs (+) represent spaces. Our decoder converts both %20 and + to spaces, handling both encoding conventions.

Can I decode an entire URL at once?

Yes, you can paste a complete URL and the decoder will convert all encoded characters back to their readable form while preserving the URL structure.

What if the encoded string contains invalid sequences?

The decoder will process valid percent-encoded sequences and leave invalid ones unchanged, so you can still read as much of the content as possible.
