What is Sentence Counter?

A sentence counter analyzes your text and counts the total number of sentences. It's a valuable tool for writers, students, and content creators who want to improve their writing readability and structure.

Our Sentence Counter not only counts sentences but also calculates the average number of words per sentence — a key metric for readability. Shorter sentences (15-20 words) are generally easier to read, while longer ones may need to be broken up.

Use this tool to analyze essays, articles, and any written content to ensure your writing is clear, concise, and easy to follow.

How to Use Sentence Counter

  1. Paste your text into the input area
  2. The sentence counter automatically detects sentence boundaries
  3. Review the total sentence count and average words per sentence
  4. Aim for 15-20 words per sentence for optimal readability

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the sentence counter detect sentences?

Our sentence counter identifies sentences by looking for terminal punctuation marks (periods, question marks, exclamation marks) followed by spaces or end of text. It handles abbreviations and decimal numbers correctly to give you an accurate count.

Can I count sentences in an essay or article?

Yes! Simply paste your essay, article, or any text into the input area. The sentence counter instantly shows the total number of sentences along with word count and paragraph count — perfect for academic writing and content editing.

Why is sentence count important for readability?

Sentence count helps you analyze readability and writing quality. Shorter sentences improve readability (aim for 15-20 words per sentence). Varying sentence length creates better flow. Tools like Flesch reading scores depend partly on average sentence length.
