Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp is the number of seconds (or milliseconds) that have elapsed since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC (the Unix epoch). It's a standard way to represent time in computing.

How do I know if it's seconds or milliseconds?

Timestamps in seconds are typically 10 digits (until 2286), while millisecond timestamps are 13 digits. Our tool auto-detects the format.

What timezone does it use?

The tool displays both your local timezone and UTC. Timestamps themselves are always UTC-based — the timezone only affects the human-readable display.

What is the year 2038 problem?

32-bit systems store timestamps as signed 32-bit integers, which max out at January 19, 2038. 64-bit systems don't have this problem. Our tool handles dates well beyond 2038.
